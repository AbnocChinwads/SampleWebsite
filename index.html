Dungeons Dragons

A brief introduction to the tabletop roleplaying game

What is it?

Dungeons & Dragons is a tabletop roleplaying game, in which you take on the persona of an adventuring character you create before your gameplay can begin (we will explore character creation in another section).

A standard practice is to run a session zero so that the player characters can meet and get used to each other. It also helps new players to get to know the system in a comfortable environment.

How do you play?

You roll various sided dice, the most synonymous of which is a D20. An icosohedral scoundrel whose typically referred to (at least in my experience) as RNGesus's ill will.

The Dungeon Master (DM) will lead you through a story in which you make the choices that will lead your group through each scenario. Sometimes you'll be negotiating with grand councils, attempting to dissuade them from war. You'll be delving into caves, forests, cityscapes and (sometimes) dungeons. Hunting for ancient treasures, and answers to the questions you raise as you travel the world you call home.
